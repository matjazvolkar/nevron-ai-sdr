★ Scaling strategy — domains, accounts, personas

The safe way to scale cold email is horizontally — more domains & accounts, not more volume per account. Each account maxes at ~33/day on Google Workspace. Each domain maxes at ~200-300/day before domain-level filters kick in.

Standard: 6 real-name accounts per domain

Every new outreach domain uses the same 6-person roster. Same names, same LinkedIn URLs, same signatures across domains. One identity per person — just multiple mailboxes.

All six roles are sales-facing or client-adjacent — credible senders for cold outreach to hotel GMs/owners.

6 is the ceiling per domain — do not go to 7+ At 6 × 33 = 198/day you sit at the top of Google Workspace's safe zone (~200–300/day domain-level ceiling). If you want a safety buffer, drop per-account limit to 30/day → 6 × 30 = 180/day. Need more volume? Add another domain, not another account.

Best names for new Nevron outreach domains

Search pattern: {verb}nevron.com or nevron{word}.com. Candidates (check availability): trynevron.com, getnevron.com, heynevron.com, usenevron.com, withnevron.com, nevron.io, nevronhq.com.

★ Duplicate personas across domains — allowed & recommended Using the same real-person name on multiple domains (e.g. rok.kokalj@nevron.net + rok.kokalj@trynevron.com) is a supported scaling pattern, not a spam signal. Each domain has independent reputation. Real people commonly hold multiple mailboxes (corporate + subsidiary + acquired brand).
⚠ The one hard rule No prospect may ever receive outreach from the same human name across two domains. If a hotel GM gets an email from rok.kokalj@nevron.net Monday and rok.kokalj@trynevron.com Wednesday, they flag both as spam → both domains burn at once.

Three ways to enforce non-overlap

  1. Geographic / ICP split (recommended for Nevron). E.g., nevron.net → EU (NL, SI, HR), trynevron.com → US (Florida). Lists naturally disjoint. Matches Nevron's two-region ICP structure.
  2. Angle split. Domain A runs Renovation + Rating angles, Domain B runs Competitor + AI. Same audience, different sequences. Dedupe on lead import.
  3. Cross-workspace suppression list. Single shared suppression file, every campaign blocks against it. Fragile — one slip and both domains catch heat. Prefer #1 or #2.

Signature consistency rule

Across all domains for the same person: same LinkedIn URL, same job title, same phone number, same profile photo. One identity, multiple mailboxes. If signatures diverge, prospects see fake personas and reputation collapses.

Optional: local-part variation for extra safety

If you want extra inbox-header distance in case of accidental list collision: rok.kokalj@nevron.net + rok@trynevron.com, or matjaz.volkar@nevron.net + matjaz@trynevron.com. Costs nothing, buys a little protection. Not required if enforcement rule #1 or #2 is in place.

3. Deliverability pattern risk — how to dodge it at scale

At 5+ domains, the infrastructure fingerprint becomes visible to Google/Microsoft filters. Known spammer signatures: all domains registered within days, same registrar, same DNS, same redirect target, same WHOIS contact, similar names. Below is how to vary the fingerprint so your domain cluster looks like organic corporate growth, not a spam farm.

SignalSpammer patternNevron mitigation
Registration timing 10 domains in 3 days Stagger 3–6 weeks between registrations
Registrar All same (e.g. Namecheap) Rotate: Cloudflare, Namecheap, Porkbun, Gandi
DNS provider All Cloudflare free tier Mix: Cloudflare, AWS Route 53, DNSimple, DNSMadeEasy
Naming logic Obvious prefix variants (try/get/hey/use-nevron) Vary: nevron.co, nevronhq.com, hotelmobilepartner.io, guestappworks.com
TLD diversity All .com Mix .com / .co / .io / .net
Redirect target All → same URL Point some to nevron.co, some to dedicated landing pages, some to nevron.co/mobile, etc.
WHOIS contact Same name on every record Different legitimate registrant per domain (still Nevron staff, just rotated)
Warmup start All warmup starts same day Stagger by 2–4 weeks
Sending IPs Shared Instantly IP pool = poisoned by other customers' spam Upgrade to Light Speed ($358) at 5+ domains for SISR dedicated IP/server
Rule of thumb Below 4 domains: don't overthink it, use Cloudflare + .com and move on.
4–7 domains: start varying registrar + DNS + naming pattern.
8+ domains: upgrade Instantly plan + full fingerprint diversification + dedicated ops.

The cheatsheet

A Domain fundamentals

  • Critical
    Use a dedicated outreach domain — never the primary corp domain.Protects nevron.co reputation. Currently: nevron.net.
  • Critical
    Domain age ≥ 30 days before first send, ideally ≥ 90 days.Fresh domains trigger spam filters instantly.
  • Important
    Register for ≥ 2 years upfront.1-year registrations are a common spammer pattern.
  • Recommended
    WHOIS not fully private — show legitimate registrant where possible.Fully private WHOIS + new domain = spam signal combo.
  • Recommended
    Consistent registrant info across all outreach domains you own.
  • Optional
    Avoid TLDs with bad reputation (.xyz, .top, .click)..com / .net / .io / .co are safe. .info is fine but slightly lower reputation than .com.

B Email infrastructure (Google Workspace)

  • Critical
    Google Workspace (Business Starter $6/user) — gold standard deliverability.Much better than self-hosted. Microsoft 365 is a fine alternative.
  • Critical
    Real named mailboxes (first.last@domain) — never info@, office@, sales@, contact@.Role accounts are a textbook spam signal.
  • Important
    Real employees behind each mailbox — 6-persona standard per domain.Rok, Simon, Matjaž, Gregor, Amadej, Nina. Same people across every new outreach domain.
  • Important
    Max 33 emails/day per account on Google Workspace — Instantly's official recommendation is 30-50/day.NOT 100 or 200. Google filters get aggressive above this.
  • Important
    Scale horizontally — more domains, not more volume per account.Ceiling per domain ≈ 200-300/day. 6 accounts × 33 = 198/day sits at the top of safe zone — don't push to 7+.
  • Recommended
    Login to Gmail web UI once a week per account.Accounts with no human activity look botted.

C DNS authentication

  • Critical
    SPF — single record, v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all.Multiple SPF records = instant fail. Upgrade ~all → -all after 2 weeks clean.
  • Critical
    DKIM 2048-bit — generated in Google Admin → Gmail → Authenticate email.1024-bit is deprecated. Publish the TXT record, then hit "Start authentication".
  • Critical
    DMARC — phased rollout: p=none → p=quarantine → p=reject.Start: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@nevron.net; pct=100. 2 weeks each phase.
  • Important
    Valid MX records pointing to Google — SMTP.GOOGLE.COM priority 1.Workspace uses single record now (not the old 5-record set).
  • Recommended
    MTA-STS + TLS-RPT — TLS enforcement + reporting.Shows mail hygiene. Host mta-sts.txt at https://mta-sts.domain/.well-known/.
  • Recommended
    DNSSEC enabled at registrar.Defense against spoofing. One click at most registrars.
  • Optional
    BIMI — brand logo in inbox (requires VMC certificate, ~$1,500/yr).Nice-to-have. Not worth it until volume justifies.

D Web presence

  • Critical
    The domain must resolve to something — blank/ECONNREFUSED is a major spam signal.Current: nevron.net doesn't respond. Fix immediately.
  • Important
    301 redirect to nevron.co (real site) — fastest, cleanest fix.Signals: legitimate business, no cloaking, one canonical brand.
  • Recommended
    Valid SSL cert on redirect host.Let's Encrypt via Cloudflare or your host — free, auto-renew.
  • Optional
    Dedicated landing page per outreach domain.Overkill unless you're running high-volume campaigns.

E Instantly configuration

  • Critical
    Enable warmup on every account for 3-4 weeks before first campaign send.warmup.limit: 10, enable_slow_ramp: true, weekday_only: true.
  • Critical
    Per-account daily limit = 33.Already configured on all 4 live accounts.
  • Important
    Sending gap: 1 minute minimum between sends from the same account.Burst sends look automated.
  • Important
    Custom tracking domain — track.nevron.net via CNAME to open.sleadtrack.com.UNPROXIED if using Cloudflare. Default Instantly tracking domain is shared = bad.
  • Recommended
    Turn off open tracking, keep link tracking only.Open-tracking pixels now hurt more than they help (Apple MPP, etc.).
  • Recommended
    Account rotation enabled across all campaigns.

F Email content

  • Critical
    Plain text only — no HTML, no images, no fancy formatting, no tracking pixels visible.Looks like a human wrote it in Gmail.
  • Critical
    Personalized first line with a hotel-specific detail — every single email.Non-negotiable per Nevron SDR rules.
  • Important
    Length: 50-125 words per email.Sweet spot for replies and spam filters alike.
  • Important
    No spam trigger words: "free", "guarantee", "act now", "limited time", "$$$", "click here".
  • Important
    Max 1 link per email, 0 on email #1 ideally.Link-heavy cold email = spam folder.
  • Recommended
    Vary subject line / opener per sequence step — A/B spintax.
  • Recommended
    Plain-text signature: name, role, company, LinkedIn URL.

G List hygiene

  • Critical
    Verify every email before send — Instantly bulk verify or mcp__instantly__verify_email.Bounce rate > 3% = ISP penalty.
  • Critical
    Drop all catch-all results — they're roulette.
  • Important
    Never re-import unsubscribes or hard bounces.Instantly's suppression list is per-workspace — check it.
  • Important
    ICP-qualified leads only (75-200 rooms, independent, owner-operated).Low-quality list = low reply rate = flagged as spam.
  • Recommended
    Honor unsubscribe within 10 business days (CAN-SPAM) / 30 days (GDPR).

H Warmup & ramp

  • Critical
    Minimum 3 weeks warmup before first real send. 4 weeks preferred.Current: alex 19 days in. Nina + Amadej need to start warmup when provisioned.
  • Important
    Ramp real sends gradually: Week 1: 10/day, Week 2: 20/day, Week 3: 33/day.Instantly slow-ramp handles this automatically.
  • Important
    Keep warmup running forever alongside real campaigns.Not just during onboarding. Preserves reputation long-term.

I Deliverability monitoring

  • Critical
    Google Postmaster Tools — register every domain.Spam rate, domain/IP reputation, authentication stats.
  • Important
    Dmarcian free tier — parses DMARC aggregate reports.Email dmarc@nevron.net → auto-forward to Dmarcian RUA.
  • Important
    Mail-Tester.com — pre-launch check, aim for 10/10.Send one test email per account before going live.
  • Recommended
    GlockApps or MXToolbox inbox placement tests monthly.
  • Recommended
    Monitor blacklists: Spamhaus, Barracuda, SORBS (MXToolbox free).

J Sending behavior

  • Important
    Send during business hours in the lead's timezone — weekday_only: true.
  • Important
    Consistent daily volume — avoid on/off patterns.33/day every weekday > 150/day in bursts.
  • Recommended
    Follow-ups: max 4-5 per sequence, 2-4 day spacing.Nevron sequence: 5 touches over 14 days — already compliant.
  • Recommended
    Stop sequences immediately on any reply (Instantly default).

K Compliance (GDPR + CAN-SPAM)

  • Critical
    Unsubscribe link in every email.Both GDPR and CAN-SPAM require it.
  • Critical
    Physical business address in footer.CAN-SPAM mandate for US sends.
  • Important
    Honor opt-out requests: 10 business days (US) / 30 days (EU).
  • Important
    Document legitimate interest basis (B2B hospitality decision-makers).Required under GDPR Art. 6(1)(f).
  • Recommended
    No misleading subject lines or sender identity.

L Red flags — never do

  • Critical
    Never send from the primary corporate domain (nevron.co).
  • Critical
    Never use purchased email lists.
  • Critical
    Never spin up 5+ domains in one week — registrar-level spam pattern.
  • Important
    Never skip warmup — "fast launch" = dead domain in 3 weeks.
  • Important
    Never use HTML emails with images/tables for cold outreach.
  • Important
    Never use shared tracking domain (Instantly default).
